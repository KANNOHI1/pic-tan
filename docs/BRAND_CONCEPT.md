# Brand Concept

Last updated: 2026-03-05

## Core concept
Pic-tan is a daily micro-habit app for kids to learn English and Japanese through cute images.

Primary concept line:
- "3 minutes a day. A cute start to a parent-child language habit."

## Product definition
- Not positioned as a "hard study app."
- Positioned as a "daily parent-child language habit."
- Parent is the buyer and trust decision-maker.
- Child is the daily user and engagement driver.

## Parent value pillars
1. Continuity: short sessions that fit busy family routines.
2. Safety: no ads, minimal external links, minimal personal data.
3. Visible progress: parents can quickly see what was reviewed and what is difficult.
4. Co-learning accessibility: usable even if the parent is not confident in English.

## Reason to believe
1. Image-first cards lower learning friction for children.
2. One-tap EN/JA switch reduces parent language burden.
3. 3-minute sessions are realistic for daily repetition.
4. Simple rating flow creates natural review loops.
5. Child-safe policy (no ads) protects focus and trust.

## Messaging principles
1. Lead with trust, then continuity, then learning outcomes.
2. Avoid exaggerated claims (no "genius", "IQ boost", etc.).
3. Promise habit formation and steady vocabulary exposure.
4. Keep tone warm, simple, and parent-reassuring.

## Candidate copy lines
- "Cute enough to open every day."
- "Three minutes a day for parent-child language time."
- "Ad-free and calm, for a child's first EN/JA words."
- "Do not force memorization. Build daily exposure."

## Canonical one-line pitch
Pic-tan is an ad-free parent-child app that builds a daily 3-minute English/Japanese vocabulary habit through cute image-based cards.

## Experience Direction (Approved)
1. Reposition from "flash card quiz" to "3-minute daily mission."
2. Put emotional reward first (cute reactions, celebration, progress stars).
3. Keep each session short, finishable, and replayable.
4. Make parent trust visible in-app (ad-free, privacy-safe, simple progress view).
5. Keep "cute felt world" consistent across home, play, and completion screens.
